Google Business Profile basics for roofers: 10 fixes that matter

Updated: 2026-01-30 • By Ajax Research Lab

If you’re a roofer, your Google Business Profile (GBP) is often your highest-converting “website”—because homeowners see it before they ever click your site. Here are 10 fixes that consistently show up in winning competitors.

1) Primary category: pick the closest match

Most roofers should use something like Roofing contractor (varies by availability). Don’t pick a vague category just because it sounds bigger.

2) Services: fill them out like a menu

Add your real services: roof repair, replacement, storm damage, inspections, gutters, etc. Keep wording simple.

3) Service area: be specific

List the actual cities you serve (Milwaukee, Wauwatosa, West Allis…). Don’t leave it as a giant radius if you’re truly local.

4) Photos: add proof, not stock

Upload a steady stream of real work: before/after, crews on site, trucks, closeups. Competitors who win tend to have “alive” profiles.

5) Business description: say what customers care about

Use plain language about speed, communication, cleanliness, and warranties. That’s what review themes usually reveal.

6) Q&A: seed the obvious questions

Use a personal account to ask common questions, then answer them on the business account (follow Google’s policies). Examples:

  • Do you offer financing?
  • Do you do emergency tarping?
  • Are estimates free?

7) Posts: once a week beats bursts

Competitors that win tend to post consistently: seasonal tips, recent jobs, storm response, maintenance checklists.

8) Reviews: respond to all of them

Short, specific responses. For negatives, take accountability and move to resolution.

9) Website link: point to a service page, not just the homepage

If your top service is roof repair, link GBP to the roof repair page. Make it easy to take action.

10) Track calls/leads (even a simple spreadsheet)

If you can’t measure, you can’t improve. Even “source = GBP” logged in a sheet helps.
