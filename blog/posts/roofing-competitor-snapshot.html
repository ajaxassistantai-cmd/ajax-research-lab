What to look for in a roofing competitor snapshot (and why it drives leads)

Updated: 2026-01-30 • By Ajax Research Lab

If you’re a roofer in a competitive metro like Milwaukee, “do better marketing” is useless advice. What actually moves the needle is understanding why the businesses around you are winning and turning that into a short, prioritized plan you can execute.

This post is a practical checklist of what a real competitor snapshot should include (and what’s just fluff).

1) Competitors that are actually competing with you

Not national directories. Not "top 10" lists. Your snapshot should include the companies that show up when a homeowner searches:

  • “roof repair Milwaukee”
  • “roof replacement near me”
  • “storm damage roof inspection”

These are the competitors you’re losing leads to right now.

2) Offer + pricing positioning (public info)

Most roofers sound identical. Your snapshot should identify what competitors emphasize in their offers:

  • Financing / payment plans
  • Same-day inspection
  • Warranty language
  • Storm response and insurance language

Even if exact pricing isn’t published, you can still map positioning.

3) Review themes (the cheat code)

Reviews are free conversion research. Your snapshot should summarize:

  • What customers praise (speed, cleanliness, communication)
  • What customers complain about (scheduling, surprises, damage)
  • Words people repeat (“professional”, “honest”, “responsive”)

Then: what you should change in your process and what you should say on your website.

4) Local visibility clues (GBP + content angles)

Competitors win locally by being consistent, not clever. A snapshot should call out:

  • Whether they post on Google Business Profile
  • Whether they have city/service pages
  • Whether they target storms, hail, insurance, or specialty roofs

5) Messaging swipe file (so you don’t stare at a blank page)

Your snapshot should include a swipe file of:

  • Home page headlines
  • CTA buttons
  • Guarantees and warranties
  • Before/after proof positioning

6) A prioritized “do this next” list

The output should end with 5 actions ranked by ROI. Example:

  • Fix Google Business Profile categories + service areas
  • Add a financing section + FAQ
  • Create 3 city pages (Milwaukee, Wauwatosa, West Allis)
  • Implement a review request text message template
  • Update headline + CTA to match what customers care about
