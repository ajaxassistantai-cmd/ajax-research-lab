The review playbook: how local roofers win (without begging)

Updated: 2026-01-30 • By Ajax Research Lab

For roofers, reviews aren’t just “reputation.” They’re lead conversion. Homeowners are nervous: big-ticket job, unknown quality, lots of scam stories. Reviews reduce fear.

What works: the simple 3-step system

  • Step 1 — Ask at the right moment: after the walkthrough, when the customer says something positive.
  • Step 2 — Make it one tap: send the review link via text/email immediately.
  • Step 3 — Follow up once: one polite reminder 48–72 hours later.

What to say (copy/paste templates)

On-site ask (spoken):

  • “Glad you’re happy with it. Would you be open to leaving a quick Google review? It helps us a lot.”

Text message (immediate):

  • “Thanks again for choosing us. If you have 30 seconds, here’s the link to leave a Google review: [LINK]. Appreciate it!”

Follow-up (48–72h later):

  • “Quick follow-up — if you meant to leave a review but didn’t get a chance, here’s the link again: [LINK]. Thank you!”

How competitors win with reviews (what to watch)

When we build competitor snapshots, the winning roofers tend to:

  • Get reviews consistently (not in bursts)
  • Respond to reviews (especially negative) calmly and specifically
  • Use review language in website headlines (“responsive”, “clean”, “on time”)

One pro tip: improve the words people use

You can’t script reviews, but you can nudge detail by asking a specific question:

  • “If you mention what we did (repair vs replacement) and how communication was, that helps.”
